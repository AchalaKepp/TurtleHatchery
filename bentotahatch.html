Bentota Hatchery

About Bentota Turtle Hatchery

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a conservation project located in Bentota, Sri Lanka, dedicated to protecting and preserving sea turtles. Situated along the stunning coastline, the hatchery plays a crucial role in safeguarding these endangered creatures and raising awareness about their plight. The facility offers visitors a unique opportunity to witness the various stages of a turtle's life cycle, from the nesting process to the release of hatchlings into the ocean. With a strong focus on education and sustainability, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery serves as a vital sanctuary for sea turtles and a beacon of hope for their future survival.

Conservation efforts

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery is committed to an array of conservation efforts aimed at safeguarding sea turtles and their habitats. The hatchery actively engages in turtle conservation through its dedicated team of experts and volunteers. They work tirelessly to protect nesting sites, monitor and relocate vulnerable nests to safe areas, and provide essential care for injured or sick turtles. Additionally, the hatchery conducts research and collaborates with local communities to promote responsible fishing practices and reduce pollution that poses a threat to marine life. Through its conservation initiatives, the Bentota Turtle Hatchery strives to create a sustainable environment for sea turtles, ensuring their survival for generations to come.
